Interactive Campbell-Stokes Sunshine Meter

This tool demonstrates the workings of a Campbellâ€“Stokes recorder, an instrument used to measure sunshine duration. The original was invented by John Francis Campbell in 1853 and modified by Sir George Gabriel Stokes in 1879.

It uses a glass sphere to focus sunlight onto a special card. As the sun moves across the sky, the focused beam burns a trace on the card. The length of this trace indicates the hours of bright sunshine.

How to Use:

  • Time of Day Slider: Drag the slider to simulate the sun moving across the sky from morning (left) to evening (right).
  • Toggle Sunshine Button: Click this to simulate clear skies (sun can burn) or cloudy skies (sun cannot burn).
  • Observe how a "burn" mark is created on the card when the sun is shining.
  • Reset Card: Clears any burn marks from the card.
